Results

Selected findings from the CADI analysis. All changes are measured in People Fed Yearly (PFY) — the number of people a grid cell's caloric output could feed for one year.

Country-Level Rankings

Which countries experience the largest aggregate caloric changes — both net and loss-only (ignoring gains within each country).

Observed net PFY change (2001–2020 vs 1981–2000): top 10 gainers and bottom 10 losers.
Same ranking normalized by population, revealing disproportionate impacts on smaller nations.
Loss-only ranking: aggregate PFY losses ignoring within-country gains. Large countries with mixed outcomes (e.g., India) rank higher here.
Projected net PFY change by 2041–2060 (SSP5-8.5). Baseline: 2001–2020.

Cropland Affected

Share of global harvested area experiencing caloric decline at different severity thresholds.

Observed period: share of harvested area with caloric losses exceeding 5%, 10%, 15%, and 20%.
Breakdown by land type: rainfed cropland is more exposed to climate-driven losses than irrigated areas.
Projected cropland affected across periods under SSP3-7.0. The share of area exceeding each threshold grows over time.
Same analysis under SSP5-8.5 — higher-emission scenario shows steeper acceleration of losses.

Latitude Patterns

How caloric changes vary by latitude — tropical regions bear the brunt of losses while higher latitudes see some gains.

Observed PFY changes by latitude band: losses concentrated in the tropics, modest gains at northern latitudes.
Projected latitude profile for 2041–2060 (SSP5-8.5): tropical losses intensify and spread poleward.
